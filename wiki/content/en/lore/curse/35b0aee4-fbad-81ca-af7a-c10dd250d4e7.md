A curse is not a trigger behavior. That is to say, it is not the so-called "if you do something, you will receive some kind of punishment."

In stories, those who are cursed due to their own actions are often cursed for violating a contract rather than because of the nature of the Curse itself.

&nbsp;

Like {{link:35b0aee4-fbad-81f7-b930-fb96af1ba775|预言}}, curses are not unbreakable. The stronger the caster's magic, or the higher the proficiency in {{link:35b0aee4-fbad-81c1-81af-d0a9afe0948b|//Execrantia}} or {{link:35b0aee4-fbad-8198-ac6f-f94810d52719|Hex}}, the more likely the curse is to become reality.

&nbsp;

A curse is a broad concept — it can refer to a form of magic, or it can be a tragedy bound by the chains of fate. Relatively speaking, {{link:35b0aee4-fbad-8142-bf13-f4676fc46608|Hillkind}} have a stronger ability to control magic, because the curse flowing in their blood is itself a form of magic. The vast majority of curses are magical, though individual counterexamples include {{link:35b0aee4-fbad-810b-bfc0-c7412c6c5109|Daemon R. Brigid}}'s {{link:35b0aee4-fbad-81c1-81af-d0a9afe0948b|//Execrantia}} (which directly manipulates fate itself).
