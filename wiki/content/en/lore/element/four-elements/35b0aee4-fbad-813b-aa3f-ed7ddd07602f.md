The Four Elements that make up the world—fire, water, wind, and mountains—were adapted from classical alchemical theory with minor modifications.

&nbsp;

There are actually five elements in Four Elements, namely the four mentioned above plus {{link:35b0aee4-fbad-81ec-bc80-e3542e080055|Element V}} and {{link:35b0aee4-fbad-8126-aa93-c422b2f64b85|Mana}}. This evolutionary relationship corresponds to Plato's theory of five elements and the four elements of Western alchemy. Over the course of long transmission, {{link:35b0aee4-fbad-8126-aa93-c422b2f64b85|Mana}} (the ether among the five elements) was forgotten, and nowadays it's commonly believed there are only four elements.
