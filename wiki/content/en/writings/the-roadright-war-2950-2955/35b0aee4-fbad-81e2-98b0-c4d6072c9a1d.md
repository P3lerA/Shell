A book written in the Common Tongue on thin, light paper, which details the entire course of {{link:35b0aee4-fbad-8101-a593-e9fc8abf6718|First Argos-Hillkind War(Roadright War I)}}. The paper has yellowed severely. The author's name has been deliberately erased.

---

&nbsp;

In this layer of historical past, the {{link:35b0aee4-fbad-8130-a5b7-fefcafe7f449|Argouses}} ultimately achieved tremendous success. The only price they paid for their previous ambiguous behavior was even greater territory. {{link:35b0aee4-fbad-8142-bf13-f4676fc46608|Hillkind}}, due to rash advance, suffered a major defeat; the stakes even devoured the greatest one in their ranks.

&nbsp;

This unnamed author mentions that {{link:35b0aee4-fbad-8156-adaa-e50b3b9d75a6|Ourea}}'s disciples hesitated about their identity, with one of them (whose name was deliberately blackened) even returning to {{link:35b0aee4-fbad-8136-9faf-c037fd931a68|Argos}} to organize an army, while the other simply stood by from the beginning.
