Scipio Hiereus (216-635), {{link:35b0aee4-fbad-819e-8714-d1d33be9a203|Eltarnern}} native, {{link:35b0aee4-fbad-81e3-b34f-f38d0c851cf9|High Elf}} Sorcerer, the first gifted of {{link:35b0aee4-fbad-81d5-a7b8-e40d8972d75e|Astreia}} among all elven races.

&nbsp;

As an adult, despite having considerable mastery over the art of controlling magic, Scipio still showed no signs of manifesting innate magic. For most of his life, he merely idly wandered along the banks of the {{link:35b0aee4-fbad-81a3-852a-ed71870ccbbf|Anduin River}}, gazing at the stars reflected in the river, extrapolating their origins and futures, until an imprudent traveler slew the God of Fate, and a falling star illuminated the night sky of Ragriff. (See the {{link:35b0aee4-fbad-81e5-a23f-c28ec83130c0|Origin}} of {{link:35b0aee4-fbad-8142-bf13-f4676fc46608|Hillkind}})

&nbsp;

That night, an elf with no destination understood the future beneath the starry sky—the calculations of countless years are not in vain; when the moment arrives, they will arrange themselves into the sigil of destiny. This is the beginning of {{link:35b0aee4-fbad-81d5-a7b8-e40d8972d75e|Astreia}}.

&nbsp;

From then on, the elves possessed a unique means of gazing into fate. Compared to the ordinary priests of other races, the elves truly "understood" fate. The clan Hiereus also occupied an irreplaceable position among the elves.
